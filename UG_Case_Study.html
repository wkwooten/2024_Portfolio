    Untermyer Gardens Responsive Web Redesign

    View Prototype

    1. Overview

    My Role

    Lead Interaction Designer & Researcher

    Collaborators

    Elvis Towers

    Sara Semmoune

    Tools

    Figma

    Adobe Creative Cloud

    The Problem

    The team noticed that the Untermyer Gardens Website had number of usablibilty issues which made it it difficult for users to nagivate the website.

    Our Solution

    Our aim is to make information more accessible and engaging through a simplified content structure.

    Background

    The Untermyer Gardens Conservancy is a 43-arce public Garden located in Yonkers, New York focused on the restoration and preservation of the historic Gardens.

    2. Research

    Research Plan

    To kick things off, we ran a heuristic evaulation on the website to determine if the site was compliant with web standards. During this evaulation, we found that the font size was 11px, which is too small for sight impaired individuals.

    We followed up by conducting an interview with a stakeholder from Untermyer Gardens. The purpose of this interview was to understand the brand and to learn of any specifications the stakeholder had.

    After this we conducted user interviews to see how they navigated the website and used that information to create empathy maps and get our user insights.

    User Interviews & Research Insights

    We conducted 5 in-person interviews in which we observed the users while they interacted with the website as they saw fit.

    After allowing a few minutes for users to explore the site and interact with the site, the team asked a series of questions pertaining to their experience with the site.

    Our research showed that users were overwelmed by the header and felt that some of the pages were too visually busy. However, we also observed that users were drawn to the pictures.

    User Persona

    We created Walter to remind us who the user is. Walter represents the everyman that simply wants to find directions with a little friction as possible.

    3. Ideation

    Feature Prioritization

    By restructuring the navigation bar, we figured it would have a high impact on helping to lessen confusion for users when trying to find a certain page

    Additionally, we wanted to ensure that the font size used a font size based on rem units

    Information Architecture & Card Sorting

    Our greatest task was to completely overhaul the nav bar to make it less cluttered and easy to understand at glance. We focused on reducing redudances and ensuring that like elements would be paired together.

    User Flow

    We created a user flow so that we could focus on delivering the features our users and stakeholders would appriecite.

    We wanted to make the process of learning about and setting up tours much easier

    4. Prototyping

    Paper Wireframing

    Mid Fidelity Mockup

    In terms of styling, the team felt it was important to maintain the feel of the orginal site. We felt that the orginal design fit well with the brand and thus we did not want to make any radical design changes. Instead, we applied design principals to establish a clear hierachy of elements.

    Based on the our research, users liked the photos on the site so we put them front and center. We felt this would be a good way to give vistors to the site a clear idea of what the park looks like.

    Additionally, the new nav bar features dropdown menus so that guests wont be overwelmed by too many options. Now, guests can easy parse the options to determine what suits them best.

    5. Testing

    Testing Plan

    With this round of testing, we wanted to focus on the usuablity and see if users could easily navigate the site with the new navigation bar.

      We conducted 3 usablibilty tests:

    Testing Insights

      Some User Pain points:

    • The most difficult task was finding the map
    • Font size was still too small
    • Wasn’t clickable on all pages
    • Main title screen needs adjustment
    • Hard to notice donation button
    • Support was not clickable on all pages
    • Events and programs was not clickable

      Users liked:

    • Thought site looks vibrant & easy on the eyes
    • Was easy to find location & hours
    • That site included temperture option

    Iteration Notes

    Based upon this feedback we ironed out the issues with the navigation and fixed the clicking issues
