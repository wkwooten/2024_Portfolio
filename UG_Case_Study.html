1. Overview

Background

The Untermyer Gardens Conservancy is a 43-acre public garden located in Yonkers, New York. The organization is dedicated to the restoration and preservation of these historic gardens.

The Problem

Our team identified several usability issues with the Untermyer Gardens website that made it difficult for visitors to navigate and find essential information.

Our Solution

We focused on making information more accessible and engaging through a simplified content structure, improving the overall user experience for visitors to the website.

2. Research

Accessibility Testing

We began with a comprehensive heuristic evaluation of the website to assess its compliance with web accessibility standards. Our evaluation revealed several issues:

User Interviews & Research Insights

We conducted five in-depth user interviews, observing how participants interacted with the website naturally. After the initial exploration period, we followed up with targeted questions about their experience.

Our research revealed that users found the header overwhelming and certain pages too cluttered with information. However, they consistently praised the quality and quantity of images throughout the site.

User Persona

We created Walter's persona to represent our typical user: someone who wants to find essential information about the gardens quickly and efficiently. This persona helped us maintain focus on solving real user needs throughout the design process.

3. Ideation

Feature Prioritization

The team decided to prioritize restructuring the navigation bar, as we felt that doing so would allow users to more easily be able to find what they are searching for.

Additionally, increase the font in order to ensure the site would be accessible.

Information Architecture & Card Sorting

Our greatest task was to completely overhaul the nav bar to make it less cluttered and easy to understand at glance. We focused on reducing redudances and ensuring that like elements would be paired together.

User Flow

We created a user flow so that we could focus on delivering the features our users and stakeholders would appriecite.

We wanted to make the process of learning about and setting up tours much easier

4. Prototyping

Mid Fidelity Mockup

In terms of styling, the team felt it was important to maintain the feel of the orginal site. We felt that the orginal design fit well with the brand and thus we did not want to make any radical design changes. Instead, we applied design principals to establish a clear hierachy of elements.

Based on the our research, users liked the photos on the site so we put them front and center. We felt this would be a good way to give vistors to the site a clear idea of what the park looks like.

Additionally, the new nav bar features dropdown menus so that guests wont be overwelmed by too many options. Now, guests can easy parse the options to determine what suits them best.

5. Testing

Testing Plan

With this round of testing, we wanted to focus on the usuablity and see if users could easily navigate the site with the new navigation bar.

    We conducted 3 usablibilty tests:

Testing Insights

    Some User Pain points:

  • The most difficult task was finding the map
  • Font size was still too small
  • Wasn't clickable on all pages
  • Main title screen needs adjustment
  • Hard to notice donation button
  • Support was not clickable on all pages
  • Events and programs was not clickable

    Users liked:

  • Thought site looks vibrant & easy on the eyes
  • Was easy to find location & hours
  • That site included temperture option

Iteration Notes

Based upon this feedback we ironed out the issues with the navigation and fixed the clicking issues
